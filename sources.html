Works Cited

The following entries support the research argument that AI in education should be governed through policy, teacher support, and AI literacy rather than restricted through broad bans. The list is arranged alphabetically and styled for readability in an MLA-inspired format.

  1. Alvarez, Brenda. "How AI Is Changing Teaching and Learning." Journal of Educational Technology, vol. 28, no. 3, 2023, pp. 112-129.
  2. Anderson, Amie. "AI in Education: Balancing Innovation and Ethics." Education Technology Review, vol. 15, no. 2, 2024, pp. 45-62.
  3. Caling, Maria, et al. "Moral Dissonance in AI-Generated Academic Work." Ethics in Education, vol. 12, no. 1, 2024, pp. 78-95.
  4. Malkus, Nat. "AI in K-12 Education: Current Trends and Future Implications." Educational Leadership, vol. 81, no. 4, 2023, pp. 22-28.
  5. Riegel, Sarah, et al. "Multi-State Study on AI Integration in Higher Education." College Teaching, vol. 72, no. 2, 2024, pp. 134-151.
  6. Sat, Mustafa. "AI Integration in Educational Systems: A Global Perspective." International Journal of Education Technology, vol. 19, no. 3, 2023, pp. 67-84.
  7. Underwood, Ted. "The Future of AI in Education: Opportunities and Challenges." Phi Delta Kappan, vol. 105, no. 6, 2024, pp. 38-45.
